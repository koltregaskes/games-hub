# Story

## What good looks like

- Story supports the player fantasy instead of delaying it.
- The opening hook is clear and fast.
- Characters and lore are sized to the current production reality.
- Narrative payoffs are tied to systems, missions, or runs.

## Minimum studio-standard version

- One clear player fantasy.
- One hook that explains why this run or world matters.
- One light cast or world frame.
- One rule for what narrative is deliberately postponed.

## Common mistakes

- Writing a universe bible before the opening ten minutes work.
- Adding more named factions and characters than the demo can support.
- Treating lore documents as a substitute for dramatic play.
- Building dialogue-heavy intros that kill momentum.

## Practical checklist

- State the fantasy in one sentence.
- State the hook in one paragraph.
- Limit named characters to what the slice can actually feature.
- Tie narrative moments to gameplay states.
- Keep future lore in a parking lot, not in the critical path.
