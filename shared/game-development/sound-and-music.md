# Sound and Music

## What good looks like

- Audio tells the player what matters without clutter.
- Music supports the identity of the game, not just the silence.
- Menus, feedback, and danger cues each have their own job.
- Placeholder audio is controlled and intentional, not random.

## Minimum studio-standard version

- Menu ambience or title music.
- Confirm, warning, and fail feedback.
- A first-pass gameplay sound layer.
- A written rule for what still uses placeholders.

## Common mistakes

- Shipping a demo with no sound and calling it polish-later.
- Using mismatched asset-pack audio with no tone control.
- Making music louder than feedback the player needs.
- Forgetting that silence can make the game feel broken.

## Practical checklist

- Define the mood of the title screen.
- Define the mood of the main gameplay loop.
- Add one clear danger cue.
- Add one clear reward cue.
- Document what should be replaced before public launch.
