# Research Prompt Packs

Use these when you want a deeper research tool or agent to refresh one category without wandering all over the studio.

## Standard research frame

Always ask for:

- current state of the area
- major changes in the last 6 to 12 months
- best-fit options by team size
- practical risks and common failures
- references worth following
- what a solo studio should do first
- what should be revisited quarterly

## Sound and music

```text
Research practical sound and music workflows for solo developers and very small game teams. Cover current tools, middleware choices, browser and desktop tradeoffs, common audio mistakes, low-overhead music approaches, implementation risks, and what should be refreshed quarterly. Focus on actionable studio practice rather than theory.
```

## Graphics and art pipelines

```text
Research graphics pipelines for solo developers and small game studios. Cover 2D and 3D options, asset sourcing, stylization strategies, performance implications, small-team workflow risks, and practical ways to create a strong visual identity without a large art staff. Include what should be revisited quarterly.
```

## Engines and engine selection

```text
Research current engine and stack choices for solo developers and small game studios, including browser, Godot, Unity, and Unreal where relevant. Explain the tradeoffs, migration triggers, support risks, platform fit, and what a team should do first when choosing the default path.
```

## Story and narrative production

```text
Research narrative production strategies for solo developers and small game studios. Focus on keeping scope under control, integrating story with gameplay loops, avoiding lore debt, and choosing the lightest narrative structure that still supports a commercial game.
```

## Marketing and discoverability

```text
Research current best practices for indie game marketing and discoverability for solo developers and small studios. Cover audience hooks, wishlists, Steam and itch strategy, content loops, festival visibility, and the biggest practical marketing mistakes to avoid.
```

## Distribution and store operations

```text
Research current distribution and store-ops best practices for indie game teams. Cover public demos, private playtests, packaging, Steam, itch, GOG, common release blockers, and what should be reviewed again whenever store policies or platform requirements change.
```

## Game-dev news source curation

```text
Research the best current sources for game development news. Prioritize engine updates, tooling, middleware, store policy, studio business, production craft, and conference coverage. Group the sources by reliability and update frequency, and note which ones are best for daily scanning versus weekly review.
```

## Gaming-news source curation

```text
Research the best current sources for general gaming news. Prioritize reliable coverage of releases, platform moves, industry developments, notable announcements, and major controversies. Group the sources by reliability and update frequency, and note which ones are best for daily scanning versus weekly review.
```
