# Launch and Store Readiness

## What good looks like

- The team can separate demo-ready, review-ready, and launch-ready honestly.
- Store-facing assets and packaging are treated as part of the product.
- Save/load, performance, onboarding, and stability are not being left for the final week.
- Every public build supports the promise shown on the store page.

## Minimum studio-standard version

- Clear launch criteria.
- A list of missing store assets.
- A packaging checklist.
- A save/load and stability note.

## Common mistakes

- Confusing an impressive prototype with a saleable product.
- Leaving tutorials and settings until right before launch.
- Treating screenshots and trailers as an afterthought.
- Launching with a build that cannot survive ordinary player behavior.

## Practical checklist

- Define what makes the current build not launch-ready yet.
- Track missing screenshots, trailer plan, and key art.
- Check performance and save/load behavior.
- Check packaging for each release channel.
- Re-run the honesty check before any public launch announcement.
