# Previews Vs Reviews

Previews and reviews do different jobs.

Confusing them is one of the fastest ways to end up with dishonest criticism.

## A preview answers

- what did we get to see?
- what seems promising?
- what still cannot be judged?

Previews are for:

- limited-access builds
- guided sessions
- unfinished slices
- first-look demos

Previews should not pretend they can deliver a final verdict. They should be curious, specific, and careful about uncertainty.

## A review answers

- what does the game actually feel like as a product or player-facing demo?
- what works?
- what fails?
- is it worth your time right now?

Reviews need:

- enough access to the core loop
- enough context to understand the game's real shape
- enough stability to make a verdict hold up

## A review in progress sits between them

This is useful when:

- the reviewer has played enough to form real criticism
- but the final live conditions are not fully testable yet

That is especially common with:

- online games
- live-service titles
- multiplayer launches
- late-breaking performance issues

## Practical studio rule

Use:

- `Preview` when the build is still proving itself
- `Review in progress` when the experience is almost scoreable but key conditions are missing
- `Scored review` when the build is coherent enough for a stable recommendation

## Why this matters for the Games News pipeline

If GameTrackDaily covers wider industry coverage too, `Games Reviews` and `Games Previews` should stay separate for the same reason:

- reviews are verdicts
- previews are expectation-setting

That split helps the public news page stay honest and helps the internal reviewer stay disciplined.
