# Internal Review Loops For Studios

An internal reviewer is useful because it simulates public judgment before the public arrives.

## What the internal reviewer should do

- act like a critic, not a cheerleader
- review the build in its current state
- produce a verdict a player could understand
- give the team one sharp next fix

## What the internal reviewer should not do

- rewrite the roadmap
- pretend a preview is a full review
- inflate scores because the team is trying hard
- replace playtesting, QA, or production management

## Recommended cadence

### Primary demo candidates

Run a proper internal demo review:

- once a week during active polish
- after any major feature or UX change
- before public captures, store work, or trailer work

### Secondary and proof-track projects

Run:

- one internal review after each big milestone
- lighter previews between milestones if needed

## Use the same output every time

The review should always return:

1. mode
2. score or no-score decision
3. verdict
4. strengths
5. weaknesses
6. breakdown
7. top next fix
8. confidence level

## Why this helps

- managers get a steady external-feeling read on progress
- game sessions get sharper feedback than vague encouragement
- the team can compare builds over time
- demos become easier to judge honestly

An internal reviewer is not there to crush morale. It is there to prevent false confidence.
